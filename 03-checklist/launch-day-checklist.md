# Launch Day Checklist

Everything you need to do on launch day to maximize your app's visibility and success.

## Before Launch Day

### T-7 Days: Preparation

- [ ] App approved and ready for release
- [ ] Landing page live at your domain
- [ ] Social media accounts created
- [ ] Product Hunt listing drafted
- [ ] Email list ready (friends, beta testers, etc.)
- [ ] Press kit prepared (logo, screenshots, descriptions)

### T-3 Days: Content Ready

- [ ] Launch tweet/thread written
- [ ] LinkedIn post written
- [ ] Reddit posts written (for relevant subreddits)
- [ ] Indie Hackers post written
- [ ] Hacker News submission ready
- [ ] Email to friends/family drafted
- [ ] Demo video recorded (60 seconds max)

### T-1 Day: Final Checks

- [ ] All monitoring set up (crash reporting, analytics)
- [ ] App works on production
- [ ] Payment processing tested
- [ ] Support email ready
- [ ] Social profiles link to app
- [ ] Friends reminded to be ready to engage

## Platform-Specific Tips

### Product Hunt

- Best launch days: Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday
- Launch at 12:01 AM PT for full 24-hour window
- Prepare a compelling tagline (60 chars)
- First comment should explain your journey
- Engage authentically, don't ask for upvotes directly
- Prepare GIF/video showing the app

### Twitter/X

- Thread format works well for launches
- Include visuals (screenshots, GIF, video)
- Tag relevant people who might share
- Use relevant hashtags: #buildinpublic #indiehacker #launched
- Reply to every comment for algorithm boost

### Reddit

- Follow each subreddit's rules carefully
- Don't post same content to multiple subs
- Be helpful, not promotional
- Respond to all comments
- Upvotes from others help visibility

### Hacker News

- "Show HN" format for technical launches
- Keep title factual, not salesy
- Prepare to answer technical questions
- Don't ask friends to upvote (they detect this)
- Best times: 9-11 AM ET

### LinkedIn

- Personal story works better than product pitch
- Include journey and lessons learned
- Ask for specific help (try the app, share feedback)
- Engage with comments quickly

## Crisis Management

### If Something Breaks

1. **Don't panic** - Issues are normal
2. Post status update if major issue
3. Fix quickly or roll back
4. Communicate transparently
5. Follow up when resolved

### If No Traction

1. Analyze what posts got engagement
2. Try different angles/messages
3. Reach out personally to influencers
4. Don't give up - momentum builds slowly
5. Plan for second launch attempt

### If Negative Feedback

1. Thank them for the feedback
2. Ask clarifying questions
3. Don't be defensive
4. Promise to consider improvements
5. Follow up when you address it
